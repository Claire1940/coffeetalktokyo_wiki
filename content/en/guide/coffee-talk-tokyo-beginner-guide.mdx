Brewing the perfect drink is central to progressing in **Coffee Talk Tokyo**. Each customer has unique tastes and emotional states that can be soothed or uplifted by the right beverage. The game's recipe system involves combining a base, a primary ingredient, and a secondary ingredient.

### Brewing Process

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose Temperature">
    Decide between **Hot** or **Cold**. This is often indicated by the customer's request or the drink's name (e.g., "Iced Hojicha").
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Base">
    Pick one of the available bases: Coffee, Tea, Green Tea, Chocolate, or Soymilk. This forms the primary flavor profile.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add Ingredients">
    Choose two additional ingredients from a wide selection (e.g., Honey, Ginger, Mint, Lychee, Ice Cream). The order matters for certain recipes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Brew and Serve">
    Once all ingredients are selected, hit the brew button. The game will animate the drink preparation. For some drinks, **latte art** might be required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Latte Art Requirement">
For certain character orders, like Ayame's Soymilk Honey Coffee on Day 1, **latte art is mandatory** to properly fulfill the order and achieve the best outcome. Always check if a specific order requires this extra step.
</Callout>

### The Tomodachill App

The in-game social media app, Tomodachill, is more than just a flavor text addition. It's a vital tool for understanding your customers' lives outside the cafe and uncovering crucial information that can influence their storylines.

| Feature | Description | Importance |
|---|---|---|
| **Character Posts** | Updates from patrons about their daily lives, struggles, and thoughts. | Provides context for their orders and emotional states. |
| **Hashtags** | Clickable tags that reveal hidden posts from characters you might not follow. | Unlocks new story layers and important clues for best endings. |
| **Recipe Book** | Stores all discovered drink recipes for easy reference. | Essential for quick brewing and memory recall. |
| **Relationship Tracker** | Shows the evolving relationships between characters and the barista. | Helps gauge your impact on their stories. |

<Callout type="info">
Regularly check Tomodachill. Liking and fully viewing specific posts, especially those under certain hashtags like #GoodGirl or #MisakiIto, can trigger new events or unlock critical information for character development.
</Callout>

### Character Interactions and Dialogue

The dialogue in **Coffee Talk Tokyo** is well-written and realistic, despite the fantastical setting. Your interactions, though limited to drink preparation and listening, subtly guide the narrative. There are no direct dialogue choices for you, the barista, but your actions (or inactions) speak volumes.

When a character asks for something vague like "any cold tea" or "something with a strong smell," you have a degree of freedom. However, for best outcomes, it's often wise to choose a drink that aligns with their personality or the ongoing narrative. For instance, a "Ginger Chocolate" for a "strong smell" request can yield positive results.
