<Section icon={Layout} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Using Stencils">

Mastering the **Coffee Talk Tokyo stencil** tool requires a bit of finesse. Unlike the freehand pouring which depends on your steady hand and timing, stencils provide a reliable way to create beautiful art every time. However, the true skill lies in knowing when to use them and how to personalize them with etching.

| Stage | Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Preparation** | Select base ingredients and brew the drink | Creates the canvas for your art |
| **Stencil Selection** | Open the stencil menu and pick a pattern | Prepares the powdery design |
| **Sprinkling** | Apply the powder over the foam or surface | Lays down the primary visual element |
| **Finishing** | Add milk or use the etching tool | Adds depth and personalization |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Brew the Perfect Base">
    Before you even think about the stencil, ensure the drink recipe is correct. Whether it is a traditional Matcha or a new Tokyo-inspired cold brew, the quality of the foam or surface determines how well the stencil sits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose Your Pattern">
    Access the stencil menu. You will find a variety of designs ranging from simple geometric shapes to more complex cultural symbols. Consider the customer's personality—a traditional kappa like Kenji might appreciate something different than the "It girl" Ayame.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Apply and Etch">
    Once the stencil is applied, do not just stop there. Use the etching tool to add flourishes around the sprinkle design. This "hybrid" approach is what separates a trainee from a master barista.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Wait for the drink to settle for a second before applying the stencil. This prevents the powder from sinking too quickly into the foam.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={PenTool} title="Creative Combinations and Customization">

The **Coffee Talk Tokyo stencil** mechanic is not just about clicking a button. It is an extension of your creative voice. By combining stencils with the traditional milk pouring and etching tools, you can create intricate designs that tell a story.

In Tokyo, the customers are looking for more than just caffeine; they are looking for a moment of peace. Your art can provide that. For example, using a floral stencil and then etching "vines" with the milk pick can create a stunning garden effect that perfectly complements the lo-fi beats of the café.

| Art Method | Best For | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stencil Only** | Quick, clean, and recognizable shapes | Easy |
| **Etching Only** | Abstract designs and flowing lines | Medium |
| **Hybrid (Both)** | High-detail, professional-grade latte art | Hard |
