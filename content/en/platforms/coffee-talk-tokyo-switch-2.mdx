<Section icon={Coffee} title="Brewing Mastery and New Mechanics">

The core gameplay of brewing remains, but **Coffee Talk Tokyo** introduces several "New Twists" to the formula. The most significant addition is the **Say it with Stencils** feature. This allows you to elevate your latte art by applying powdery designs before adding milk or etching. This adds a layer of creative expression that was previously limited to free-hand pouring.

**How to Master the Brewing Process:**

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Base">
    Choose from coffee, tea, green tea, chocolate, or milk. Each base has unique bitterness and sweetness levels.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add Ingredients">
    Mix in secondary and tertiary ingredients like ginger, mint, or lemon to alter the flavor profile.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Apply Stencils">
    Select a stencil design to sprinkle a powdery pattern on top of the drink.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finish with Etching">
    Use the milk pour or etching tool to customize the art further before serving.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Ingredient | Primary Effect | Secondary Effect | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Green Tea** | High Bitterness | Low Sweetness | Traditional characters like Kenji |
| **Ginger** | High Warmth | Spicy Kick | Customers seeking refuge from the rain |
| **Honey** | High Sweetness | Smooth Texture | Sweet-toothed characters like Ayame |
| **Mint** | Refreshing | Cooling Effect | Combatting the Tokyo summer heat |

<Callout type="tip" title="Latte Art Tip">
Using stencils can satisfy specific aesthetic requirements from customers, which helps build your relationship on the Tomodachill network faster.
</Callout>
